Tangents and Normals

The tangent line to a curve at a given point is the straight line that “just touches” the curve at that point. — Wikipedia (edited)

The tangent has the same slope that the line has at that point.

The normal line to a curve at a given point is the line perpendicular to the tangent line at that point. — Wikipedia (editied)
